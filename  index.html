ECCV Workshop Proposal

Event-Based Multimodal Vision

From Imaging to Perception and Understanding

Bridging event-based sensing with multimodal perception and foundation models. (Workshop is conditional on acceptance.)

Benchmark Challenge Organizers
Venue: ECCV 2026 (TBD)
Format: Full-day workshop
Estimated attendance: 200–300

Highlights

  • Sensor-aware multimodal pipeline (hardware → imaging → perception → understanding)
  • Focused benchmark challenges for fair comparison
  • Connect event vision with multimodal foundation models

About

Frame-based vision can fail under fast motion and drastic illumination changes. Event cameras provide asynchronous sensing, high temporal resolution, and high dynamic range, enabling robust imaging and perception in challenging conditions.

This workshop aims to bridge event-based sensing with mainstream multimodal vision, including multimodal fusion and foundation models, and to connect low-level imaging with high-level understanding.

Scope & Topics

Sensing Systems & Hardware

  1. Event-based multimodal sensing systems
  2. Multi-sensor spatiotemporal calibration
  3. Event-based multimodal datasets

Low-Level Signal & Imaging

  1. Event-guided illumination modeling
  2. Broad-range enhancement under varying lighting
  3. Illumination-aware representations with events

Mid-Level Representation & Perception

  1. Geometric / dynamic representations
  2. Multimodal event-based segmentation
  3. Detection & tracking from events

High-Level Knowledge & Understanding

  1. Event-based MLLMs
  2. Event-based visual grounding
  3. Multimodal spatiotemporal reasoning
